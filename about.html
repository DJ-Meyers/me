Who is DJ?

I'm a 22-year-old student currently attending the University of Florida where I spent my first two years studying Materials Science. After my first two years of Materials Science, I realized that I didn't enjoy Chemistry as much as I thought I did, and frankly I wasn't great at it. When considering my options for a major change, I thought about the only class I enjoyed in Materials Science - my FORTRAN programming class. I decided to switch my major to Computer Science, because it combines my love of language, grammar, and structure with my passion for problem-solving and mathematics. I'm currently completing my 4th year of university and my 2nd year of Computer Science.

I'm passionate about science outreach and have spent the last two summers working as a counselor at the University of Florida's Student Science Training Program, where I've served as a mentor to 179 high school students. I worked closely with a group of 13 students each year, advising them on their research and guiding them as they wrote a paper about their findings. As someone who participated in the program in 2011, I wanted to give back to a program that helped me discover my passion for science. I've always loved being a mentor, whether it was offering free tutoring during lunch in high school, or making an impact on students at UF's SSTP. I hope to continue to grow as a mentor in the future as a project manager or technical lead.
